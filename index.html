      Can you mine Bitcoin with a home computer?

      Technically, yes you can. Is it worth it? No.

      The days of mining bitcoin with a home computer's CPU or GPU are long gone due to the massive price increase and network difficulty. These days you need special hardware called ASICs to mine Bitcoin directly. ASICs are very expensive, very loud, produce a lot more heat, and use a lot more electricity. ASICs are typically not geared towards a home mining operation.

      As the price of Bitcoin rose from mere pennies in 2010 to a high of nearly $20,000 in December 2017, so too has the mining difficulty with many people jumping on board. Nowadays, the general practice is to mine alt-coins and exchange them for bitcoin. Bitcoin has the highest market cap, and is the most sought after coin. Bitcoin is the oldest and most valuable crypto currency. Check out CoinGecko to see a a list of crypto currencies by market cap. You do not have to exchange the alt coin you mine for Bitcoin. You can hold on to it.

      Plenty of new GPU miners who are just starting out typically point their mining rig to Nicehash which will use an algorithm to mine the most profitable alt-coin on their platform and automatically exchange it for Bitcoin. At the time of this writing --August 2020, the minimum payout on Nicehash was 0.001 BTC with free transfers if you have a coinbase account linked. to exchange it. Typically a current gen 6-8 GPU "rig" can earn the minimum payout on a daily basis. If you have a gaming desktop with 1 or 2 GPUs you would get payed once or twice a week

      Once you gain experience earning bitcoin, accumulating, transfering, and cashing out to fiat ($ US Dollar), then more experienced miners tend to mine directly on mining pools, which we will talk more about later. However, there is nothing wrong with staying on Nicehash, many people also prefer to stay on the platform for ease of use. There is zero fee for exchanging to bitcoin, and they have an app for IOS and Android where you can monitor all your rigs from one location.

      Take a look at the charts below. As of August 2020, Bitcoin and Ethereum are 2 of the highest gaining assets this decade. 11,000% and 55,000% gains. This is only counting them at current (August 2020) market price and not even their all time highs! No stock on Wall Street or other investment asset performed better in the last 10 years!

      Is GPU Mining Profitable?

      This depends on a lot of different factors, but the biggest one is your electric cost. A popular site whattomine.com, can calculate your earnings after electricity cost depending on what coin you mine.

      Remember, this does not factor in the cost of the hardware. The short answer; do not expect this to be a get rich quick investment. You will invest in the hardware, electricity cost, and time to maintain and look for profitable coins. A lot of times, especially with the top 25 coins, it is much easier to just buy the coins if you just want some skin in the game. Most GPU miners I have met, either enjoy tinkering around with hardware, or are mining a very new coin you cannot buy and taking a chance it will be worth more in the future, or they have access to very cheap electricty (solar, college dorms, included in rent. cheap electric rates under 10 cents/kwh etc...) If you are thinking you are going to buy a few computers and GPU's, put them together, and press start and money will start to appear, then you will be very dissapointed.

      Just like anything else you invest in, evaluate your cost and tolerance to risk. Can you put a PC together and troubleshoot software and hardware? Are you willing to spend the time researching a new coin project and creating a new wallet for every new coin, and signing up for a new exchange? Are you financially able to afford the parts, and the electricity cost, out of pocket, say for example if the market is down when your bills come due? Do you have already have a gaming desktop PC with one or two GPU's that you just want to mine a few hours a day and slowly accumulate something like Ethereum for long term hold?

      The way I have looked at it, is people spend their disposable income on hobbies and luxuries like cars, clothing, shoes, gadgets etc... that have zero potential of having a Return On Investment (ROI). That Luxury car or Iphone will never sell for more than you paid for it, in fact it will be worth much less as time passes. The GPUs that you purchase have the potential to give you a Return on Investment, and not to mention they are highly sought after parts for PC Gamers. Do a google search on GPU shortages during cryptocurrency bull runs (price increases).

      Bottom line, GPU mining CAN be profitable, depending on your investment of time and money, as well as your electricity cost. It can also be very expensive as well. GPUs can cost between $300 to $1,200 each. The components needed besides the GPU can be around $400-$600 as a rough estimate. A rig typically has 6-8 GPUs, you do the math. Are you willing to invest that kind of money with no guarantee that you will make a profit? Also these machines will produce a lot of heat, which you will either have to move or cool. That also comes with a cost. The fans are a static sound but very noticeable. Do your living arrangements and co-inhabitors allow for this?
