Wallets and Exchanges

You are going to need a crypto currency wallet to direct your mining earnings to. Exchanges can also recieve your mining earnings, but it is not advisable to leave large sums on an exchange. Not all exchanges will accept mining earnings either, so best to double check or you will lose what you mined. It is recommended to have a wallet that YOU hold the keys to. Once you store these keys in a safe place (the safest being your mind), then you can access your wallet and it's funds from anywhere.

In recent years, most of the major wallets and exchanges have implemented something called KYC or "know your customer". Meaning you will need identification and maybe a background or type of credit check. As Bitcoin and crypto currency have become more mainstream and known, opening a wallet or signing up for an exchange is becoming more and more like opening a bank account. Read everything carefully and consider the implications before signing up.

Wallets

We use and reccomend the following wallets:

  • Coinbase

    • Official Site
    PRO: Can buy crypto with debit, credit, bank account and wire transfer. Can sell crypto and recieve USD directly in your checking account. Website and Mobile app.
    PRO: Can exchange most of their supported cryptos with each other from within the app. Access to their Coinbase Pro platform using the same credentials, where they have a very robust trading platform.
    PRO: The largest and most established exchange in the US. They have been around since 2012. Fully registered to do business, and headquartered in San Francisco, California, USA.
    CON: Fees are on the higher side if you are not using their Coinbase Pro platform.
    CON: The app and site have crashed multiple times in the past during high volume or high volatility.
    CON: They used to have a visa debit card that worked anywhere visa was accepted including ATM's, and it auto-exchanged your bitcoin into US Dollar to cover your purchase dynamically. They have phased out this card recently. The quickest way to cash out now is via bank transfer, which takes a business day or two. **You do not own the keys to your wallet, you are trusting Coinbase 100%.

  • Trust Wallet

    • Official Site
    PRO: You have the keys to your wallet. You can recover your wallet on any device that can run trust wallet as long as you know your 12 word phrase.
    PRO: No KYC.
    PRO: Nimiq is listed on this wallet.
    PRO: Owned by Binance, the largest crypto currency exchange in the world.
    CON: Does not link to your bank account, so you cannot cash out to US Dollars. You would have to send and/or exchange your crypto to another wallet that is capabale (example: coinbase).
    CON: Mobile Only, no web version.

  • Crypto.com

    • Official Site
    PRO: Can buy crypto with debit, credit, and bank account. Can sell crypto and recieve USD directly in your checking account. Comes with a visa card that can be used anywhere visa is accepted.
    PRO: Can earn interest on Crypto, and also earn points with their cards. Can use multiple fiat, not just USD.
    PRO: Big up and coming company with a lot of positive reviews.
    CON: Fairly new company not as established as the others.
